About Christ School Bundibugyo

Our history, mission, values, and the passionate people who make CSB a transformative place to learn and grow.

Our Background

Two Decades of Education in the Mountains

Christ School Bundibugyo was founded in 2002 by missionaries and Ugandan community leaders with a shared vision: to bring world-class Christian education to one of Uganda's most underserved districts, nestled in the dramatic Rwenzori mountain range of western Uganda.

Bundibugyo District faces significant challenges — geographic isolation, limited infrastructure, and scarce educational resources. CSB was born as an answer to this need. Over two decades, the school has grown from a handful of students into a thriving boarding community of over 700 learners from Senior 1 through Senior 6.

Today, CSB is recognized across the region for its academic standards, its strong spiritual culture, and its commitment to developing the whole person — equipping graduates not just for university, but for life in service to their families, communities, and country.

Our Vision

To be an academically excellent senior secondary boarding school producing servant leaders for the good of Bundibugyo and God's glory.

Our Mission

To educate and raise up Christ-centered disciples in Bundibugyo through professional development, spiritual formation, and infrastructure investment.

Our Culture

What Makes CSB Different

CSB is more than a school — it is a family, a community, and a movement. Our culture is shaped by three foundational commitments.

🎓

Academic Excellence

We hold high standards across all subjects. Our A-Level science programme prepares students for Uganda's most competitive universities, with a focus on medicine and engineering to meet the region's most urgent professional needs.

✝️

Spiritual Formation

Daily chapel, weekly church, Scripture study, and a campus culture of prayer cultivate students who are not just academically capable, but grounded in faith and guided by Christian values in every area of life.

🤝

Community Partnership

Our Board of Governors is made up primarily of Ugandans — local officials, church elders, business leaders, and parents — ensuring CSB stays rooted in and accountable to the Bundibugyo community it serves.
